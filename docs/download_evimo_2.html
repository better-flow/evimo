Recording Setup

Our sensor rig is shown in the image; 2 Prophesee Gen3 (DVS) (640×480) are set up in a stereo configuration (we provide extrinsic and intrinsic calibration with the data). A Samsung DVS Gen3 (640×480) is in the lower middle of the rig. All event cameras are covered with IR cut filters to avoid interference with Vicon motion capture system. Additionally, an RGB camera (Flea3 USB3) is used to provide classical image frames. All four cameras are spatially and temporally calibrated and record simultaneously.

NPZ versus TXT

Decompressed, the npz format requires 525 GB of space of which 271 GB is for the RGB camera and 255 GB is for the three DVS cameras.

Decompressed, the txt format requires 842 GB of space of which 212 GB is for the RGB camera and 631 GB is for the three DVS cameras.

The npz format supports memory mapping the event arrays from disk, which saves considerable resources.

The txt format requires parsing the events.txt file, which takes considerable resources

The txt format decompresses to about 471,000 files. The npz format only requires about 4700 files.

Structure from Motion / Object Recognition

The sequences feature a variety of objects scattered on the tabletop, but the scene is rigid (no independent motion). The objects provide depth variation and some degree of diversity from recording to recording, and additionally allow to evaluate object recognition algorithms (pixelwise masks are provided).
